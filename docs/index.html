Why Cliphoard

Everything a clipboard manager should do — nothing it phones home about.

A native Swift app — no Electron, no account, no cloud. Just a fast bar for everything you copy.

⌨️

Summon from anywhere

A global ⌃⌥⌘V hotkey brings the bar up over any app. Pick a clip and it pastes straight back where you were.

✨

Semantic search

On-device AI (CoreML) finds clips by what they mean — four swappable model tiers, up to EmbeddingGemma. Search "that database query" and get it — no exact words needed.

🔒

Private by design

Everything stays on your Mac. No network calls, no telemetry, no account. Password managers are skipped automatically.

🎨

17 themes

From a clean Swiss greyscale to One Dark, Dracula, Tokyo Night, Catppuccin and more — plus light themes. Follows your system appearance.

🧱

Three layouts

A horizontal card strip, a dense compact list, or a Spotlight-style palette with a live preview. Your call.

🖼️

Text, links, colors, images

Cliphoard recognises what you copy — rich text, URLs, hex colors, files and images — and keeps thumbnails for fast browsing.

🧭

Filter by dimension & time

Every clip is auto-classified along 10 meaning dimensions. Combine filters like credential + terminal, then narrow to yesterday — or type when:today in the bar.

Search that gets you

Find clips by meaning, not exact words.

An on-device model embeds everything you copy, so plain-English queries find the right clip — even when it shares no words with what you typed. Runs entirely on your Mac.

Neural“revert my last commit” → git reset --soft HEAD~1
Smart“catchy” → “Copy anything. Hoard everything.”

Four model tiers. You pick the trade-off.

From a 2.5M-parameter featherweight to EmbeddingGemma — all on-device, all swappable in Settings. Heavier tiers install themselves the moment you pick them; nothing to download by hand. Benchmarked on a clipboard-realistic retrieval set (methodology).

Slice your hoard by meaning — and by time.

Every clip is classified along 10 dimensions (content type, domain, intent, sensitivity, source…) with 10 values each — filter any combination instantly. And time reads like you think: Today, Yesterday, This week, a custom range, or just type when:yesterday or when:2026-07-01..2026-07-15 straight into the search bar.

Built to hold secrets

Your clipboard holds passwords. We treat it that way.

Everything you copy is encrypted on disk, the key never leaves your Mac, and your clips never touch a network. Not marketing — it's how the code works.

🔐

Encrypted at rest

Every clip — text, rich text, file paths, colors, images and the semantic search index — is sealed with authenticated AES-GCM before it's written to disk. Run strings on the database and you get nothing.

🔒

Key sealed in the Secure Enclave

The key is derived from a P-256 key generated inside your Mac's Secure Enclave — its material can't be extracted. Copy the database to another machine and it's undecryptable; only this Mac can re-derive the key. No Touch ID prompt.

🙈

Secrets skipped at capture

Copies from password managers, and pasteboards marked transient or concealed, are never stored in the first place — and you can add your own apps to the skip list.

📵

Your clips never touch a network

No telemetry, no account, no cloud, no phone-home. The app's only network call is fetching an optional search model from GitHub when you pick a heavier tier — a one-time, download-only request that carries none of your data.

🗝️

Owner-only on disk

The store lives in your Application Support folder with 0700/0600 permissions — other user accounts on the Mac can't read it.

🔎

Open source, auditable

MIT-licensed. Don't take our word for it — read Crypto.swift yourself.
